# Perturb-Seq: Dissecting Molecular Circuits with Scalable Single-Cell RNA Profiling of Pooled Genetic Screens

**Authors:** A. Dixit et al. (senior: A. Regev)
**Year:** 2016
**Venue:** Cell 167:1853-1866

## Abstract

The original **Perturb-seq** paper: combines pooled CRISPR screens with scRNA-seq
via expressed guide barcodes. Applied to transcription factors and other
regulators governing the response of **mouse bone-marrow-derived dendritic cells
(BMDCs)** to **lipopolysaccharide (LPS)**, plus cell-cycle and other programs.

## Key contributions

- Established the Perturb-seq method (pooled CRISPR + scRNA-seq, guide barcodes).
- Mapped TF control of the innate-immune (LPS) response in mouse myeloid cells.
- Showed recovery of gene signatures, cell states, and regulatory interactions.

## Methods

CRISPR knockout with expressed barcodes; ~200K cells; analysis frameworks for
attributing transcriptional effects to perturbations.

## Key results

- Demonstrated feasibility and accuracy of single-cell pooled screens.
- Recovered known innate-immune regulators and their target programs.

## Why it matters for our work

⭐ **Best species + lineage match.** **Mouse**, **myeloid/innate-immune**
(dendritic cells), **LPS** response — the closest overlap with our mouse
macrophage challenge on both species and biology. Foundational reference for the
assay itself.

## Limitations / open questions

- Older and **smaller** than modern genome-scale screens.
- CRISPR **knockout** (not CRISPRi knockdown) — modality differs slightly.
- Dendritic cells, not macrophages (related myeloid lineage).
